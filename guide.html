Top 3 Aesthetic Places in the Capital of Germany

1. Berlin Cathedral

The cathedral in Berlin reaches a height of 98 m. Date of foundation – the end of the XIX century. The Neo-Renaissance architectural style was chosen for the building. The interior is very beautiful: hand-painted stained glass windows, statues of biblical characters, walls of white marble with a large number of icons. In the center of the main hall is an ancient altar. On the territory of the cathedral there is a crypt, in which 80 persons of royal blood found shelter.

You can visit the cathedral in Berlin from 9: 00 to 18: 00. You will have to pay €7 for the entrance ticket. For children from 3 to 18 years — €5. Up to 3 years-admission is free. The audio guide in Russian will cost another €4. You can buy a ticket on the spot or online on the site of the attraction.

2. Old National Gallery (Alte Nationalgalerie)

Connoisseurs of classical fine art should definitely visit the Nationalgalerie in Berlin. The National Gallery has existed in the German capital for 157 years, representing a unique collection of art works of the XIX-XXI centuries. Today, the entire collection is housed in 5 buildings, separating art objects from different centuries. The art of the XIX century, including the masterpieces of such famous artists as Adolf von Menzel, Edouard Manet, Claude Monet, is collected in the Old National Gallery on Museum Island.

3. Gendarmenmarkt

This is the name of the square in the center of the German capital. A similar Roman landmark, the Piazza del Popolo, is taken as a model. Gendarmenmarkt was founded in the middle of the XVII century as the main place of street trade. Then it was called "The Fake market". In the middle of the XVIII century, the square was built up with compact three-story buildings. On the north side of the Gendarmenmarkt is the French Cathedral, on the south-the German Cathedral. There are also many hotels, shops, cafes around. Berlin's most popular Christmas market opens here in November.
